A very brief history of C#

C# is a medium level, strongly typed, object oriented language developed by Microsoft at the turn of the century. Leading the team was Anders Hejlsberg who took a lot of what made Java great, most notably the Java Virtual Machine and improved upon some of the other design elements that had become dated. As both languages developed further they branched in different directions as each company better defined their visions for their respective languages.

C# is a language specifically designed to take advantage of the .NET framework; a robust collection of libraries with wide-ranging uses.

Why C#?

C# syntax is very easy to read which makes it ideal for beginners. It is also one of the most widely used languages in the world with a wide range of versitility. ASP.NET framework is only second to PHP for backend web development. It is also the standard language for development in the Unity game engine which is becoming increasingly popular amonst developers. And with Microsofts procurement of Xamarin C# has also become an excellent language for developers in the mobile arena.
